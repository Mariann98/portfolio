Gift from afar

My role: UX & UI Designer
Project type: Bespoke Design course

Gift from afar is one of my projects for the University course Bespoke Design. The purpose of this was to design a digital gift-giving experience for people living far away from friends and family tuned to the personality, interests, and needs of a specific user.

Problem statement

Foreign college students like myself are separated from their loved ones and this can be hard especially during special occasions, like birthdays. I want to create a fun digital gift-giving experience to help the giver and receiver build a deeper connection with each other and make the distance between them easier.

User persona

To get a clear overview of the target user I created a persona based on my findings to effectively represent their needs and characteristics of them. I continuously placed myself in Maya's "shoes" to empathize with her throughout the design process.

Storyboard

After iteration, I created a storyboard to visually predict and explore my users' experience with the gift giving-experience app.

The story of gift-giving

It’s Maya’s 20th birthday, and she receives a message from her sister. Maya downloads and opens the Gift from Afar app to see further instructions.

After scanning her ticket by the enterence she heads straight to the Goliath line and follows the guidelines and taps on the rocket. Maya is a bit confused after reading there will be another gift for her since she thought the present was the ticket to the theme park.

A screen appears welcoming her. She gets magic mittens from the instructor and puts them on. Then she waits a few minutes till the music starts playing and the ride starts. The rollercoaster takes off, Maya’s anticipation builds up as she gets her first command to look to her left to catch a doughnut. She sees the doughnut and sticks her hand to the left to touch the hologram. She touches the hologram doughnut with her mittens resulting in her feeling a buzz. She gets thrilled as she gets the taste of what she can expect. The app congratulates her on her good job.

As the roller coaster speeds up, Maya sees the message telling her to enjoy the ride. She gets an adrenaline rush all over her body as the Goliath goes down at a fast speed. Another screen comes up telling her to put her hands up to catch a cookie. Maya puts her hands up and is ready to touch the next hologram. She misses it but has the opportunity to catch it again. 3... 2... 1... she touches it and can feel the buzz again in her mittens! This continues a few times over till all 10 elements have passed. As the ride ends, Maya is sad because the fun part is over.

The screen turns festive and congratulates Maya on her achievement. She totally forgot about the actual gift that her sister got her and is extremely surprised. Maya opens it immediately and sees a ticket appear. It's an invitation to her favourite local restaurant in the Netherlands, and her sister will be there with her next week. Maya’s eyes tear up because she is happy to receive such a thoughtful gift as well that her sister will be coming to visit her.

As Maya is walking home, she wants to have another look at the gift. She opens the app and finds out that the gift can be saved in a digital toaster which makes her smile. Maya notices another function and taps on it. Pictures of her catching the snacks appear and she starts laughing. She downloads the photos and sends them to her sister thanking her for the experience.
