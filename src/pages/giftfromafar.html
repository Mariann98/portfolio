Gift from afar

Gift from afar is one of my projects for the University course Bespoke Design. The purpose of this was to design a digital gift-giving experience for people living far away from friends and family tuned to the personality, interests, and needs of a specific user. This project was made in teams of two.

Problem:

Receiving personalized gifts from friends and family is not possible for students studying abroad.

Solution:

A digital gift-giving experience combining the user's passion for food and adrenaline.

Step 1 Research set-up

In week 1 I had to meet with my user and have an unofficial meeting in order to get to know one another. This followed with coming up with research questions, set-up, and techniques. In week 2 I conducted a semi-structured interview, shadowing, and a collage according to my research set-up.

Step 2 Data overview

In week 2 I gathered all the data I got from my research techniques and made a table overview of them. Afterward, I placed them on Miro to create clusters and insights.

Step 3 Surprise for the User

Week 3 aligned with the Dutch traditional Sinterklaas day, therefore I had to make a 'surprise' for my user. Sinterklaas gifts are to be creatively disguised and are usually accompanied by a humorous poem that has something to do with the character or a characteristic of the receiver. I had to get my user a present that she would like and pack it in a cool way that would represent her characteristics. During my research I found out that she loves food and especially Nutella and is passionate about photography, I got a good sense of what to make for her.

Step 4 Persona and Design Vision

In week 4, my partner and I had to combine our User Portraits into one Persona and come up with a design vision.
Maya is a traveling soul since she has had the opportunity to explore new countries and has also lived in a few of them throughout the years. She loves to be surrounded by her friends and family despite being introverted. Her passions are photography, gaming, and eating together with her loved ones which is a nice way for them to bond with each other. Because of her constant moving, she often gets separated from her friends and needs to find new alternatives to connect with them and have some quality time together.

Explanation for the intangible gift: The user loves food and thinks it is one of the best presents especially when it is for free. Also,this way the user can have some quality time with her sister which she really appreciates.

Gift: invitation to user's favorite restaurant

Gift-giver: Sister

Occasion: Birthday Show of caring: puts effort into the wrapping by creating an interesting and emotional experience.

Design direction: A meaningful real-life imitation of gift-giving
The experience we want the user to have should imitate the feeling she has with her loved ones, but remotely from wherever she is. We want her to have a surprise moment when receiving the present, giving her a sense of recognition from others reaching out to her. Maya is a person who loves to keep meaningful gifts in a box/on the wall, so there should be a possibility to preserve those significant gifts in a cool way online.

Step 5 Ideation

In weeks 6 and 7, my team had to come up with HMW questions and use them to conduct testing by using creative techniques. We chose Worst idea, Brainwrite, and Dot voting methods. Afterward, we had to do braindump ourselves by coming up each with 40 ideas and then sketching 20 of them. This followed with paper prototypes, and three draft concepts per team member.

Step 6 Storyboard

After ideation, I created a storyboard to visually predict and explore my users' experience with the gift giving-experience app.
