SportFlex

Sportflex is a place for fitness, where young people can connect with friends. This idea was brought to me by my brother who participated in Hack The Crisis: Youth Hackathon with his teammates and won the 2nd price. My job for this project was to design a fun and simple look for this app.

Problem: Unable to work-out with friends

Working out can be a fun social activity, but due to the current situation in the world, it is not possible.

Solution: Work-out challenge app

We want to ease the problem of not being able to do sports together or in the way we're used to. The app simplifies the social experience of sport.

User persona

To get a clear overview of the target user I created a persona based on my findings to effectively represent their needs and characteristics of them. I continuously placed myself in Kelly's "shoes" to empathize with her throughout the design process.

Lo-fi wireframes & research

After reading through my brothers findings I started creating wireframes to cover my users needs.
During the research phase, the Hackathon team send out a link to their social media friends and asked “How many squats can you do compared to me?” In just about an hour over 50 people answered. In addition, one of the teammate’s videos called his friends and challenged them to do a squats competition, which everyone accepted.

Have an overview of your friends and challenges. By simply clicking on the trophy, you can motivate your friends to be active. Maybe checking your friends results can boost you to accomplish new streaks.
